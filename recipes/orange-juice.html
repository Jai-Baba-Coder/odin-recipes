Ingredients

  • Oranges
  • Water
  • Ice Cubes (Optional)
  • Sugar or Sweetener (Optional)

Steps

  1. Wash the Oranges: Rinse the oranges under cold running water to remove any dirt or debris from the skin.
  2. Cut the Oranges: Use a sharp knife to cut the oranges in half crosswise.
  3. Juice the Oranges: Squeeze the juice from each orange half using a citrus juicer, reamer, or your hands. Apply pressure and twist the fruit to extract as much juice as possible.
  4. Strain the Juice (Optional): If you prefer pulp-free juice, strain the freshly squeezed juice through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth to remove any pulp or seeds.
  5. Dilute the Juice (Optional): If you find the orange juice too strong, you can dilute it with water to your desired taste. Start by adding a small amount of water and adjust to your preference.
  6. Sweeten the Juice (Optional): Taste the orange juice and add sugar, honey, or another sweetener if desired. Stir well to dissolve.
  7. Chill the Juice (Optional): If you prefer your orange juice cold, refrigerate it for at least 30 minutes before serving, or add ice cubes to individual glasses.
  8. Serve and Enjoy: Pour the freshly squeezed orange juice into glasses and serve immediately. Garnish with orange slices or mint leaves for an extra touch.
