Ingredients

  • Tea Powder
  • Water
  • Milk (Optional)
  • Sweetener (Optional)
  • Flavourings (If needed)

Steps

  1. Boil Water: Heat water in a kettle until it reaches the desired temperature. The ideal temperature can vary depending on the type of tea you're making. For example, black tea is usually brewed with water at or near boiling point, while green tea is typically brewed with slightly cooler water to prevent bitterness.
  2. Prepare Tea Leaves or Tea Bags: If you're using loose tea leaves, measure out the desired amount according to your taste preferences. If using tea bags, simply take out the required number of bags.
  3. Place Tea in Cup: Put the tea leaves or tea bags into a teapot or directly into the cup you'll be using to brew the tea.
  4. Pour Water: Once the water has reached the appropriate temperature, pour it over the tea leaves or tea bags.
  5. Steep: Allow the tea to steep for the recommended amount of time. This can vary depending on the type of tea and your personal preference. Typically, black tea steeps for 3-5 minutes, green tea for 1-3 minutes, and herbal tea for 5-7 minutes. Steeping too long can result in a bitter taste.
  6. Remove Tea Leaves or Bags: After steeping, remove the tea leaves or tea bags from the cup. If using tea bags, you can simply take them out. If using loose leaves, you may need to strain the tea as you pour it into another cup.
  7. Add Sweetener (Optional): If desired, add sweetener such as sugar, honey, or artificial sweetener to taste. Stir well to dissolve.
  8. Add Milk or Cream (Optional): If you like your tea with milk or cream, you can add it now. The amount you add can vary depending on your preference.
  9. Add Flavorings (Optional): If you want to enhance the flavor of your tea, you can add optional flavorings such as lemon slices, mint leaves, or ginger.
  10. Enjoy: Your tea is now ready to be enjoyed! Sit back, relax, and savor the flavor.
